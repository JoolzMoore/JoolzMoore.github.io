Emotional intelligence


Date: 2 April Updated: 28 May 2016

What is emotional intelligence and how it is developed?
t can be developed by paying attention to what is happening in the brain, and by moderating responses and eventually changing the way the brain works. All emotional states have a physiological core, so we can understand them and be in control physically as well as mentally. Emotional intelligence is about refining emotions, and making the brain and body work together in a regulated and controlled way.

Benefits of emotional intelligence
Overall outcomes include: better work performance, building leadership qualities, and the ability to create your own happiness, with long-term practise. Emotional intelligence will create the ideal conditions which ultimately can help take on challenges, build self-confidence, increase analytical ability and clear the way for more conceptual thinking.

Techniques and practices for improving emotional intelligence
The brain is active and has built-in nueroplasticity, so it has the inherent ability to change and grow. If we pay attention to changes, and foster a growth mindset, the brain will follow. How? There is a section of the brain – the amygdala – that is influenced by sensations to the body. Emotions have an identifiable autonomic or bodily change that is detected in our brain, so that gut feeling really can be a felt sensation. So can only use this to be attentive and isten to all physiological and physchological changes and be able to regulate triggers with appropriate responses.

How to develop a basic mindfulness practice?
Finding an attentional focus - looking at the way you view yourself. It begins with self-processing emotions and re-ordering brain systems, to help recognise what mode you are in, and to watch subtle changes in emotion. The ability to modulate and to have clarity allows us to be in the present in a non-judgemental way.
