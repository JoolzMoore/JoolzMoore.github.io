Daily mindfulness


Date: 27 April Updated: 26 June 2016

What did you learn from your 'mindfulness conversation'? How did the other person find it?
I have practised this before in a peer mediation course, in order to be more fully present within a conversation and to help concentrate on anothers position and situation. It helps form a bond with people as you link with them and truly feel more about their ideas, putting yourself in their shoes. It also helps reduce your own stress whilst you focus on others. I tried it with my husband and he truly though something was up when I said can we sit down and talk after dinner. I usually scuttle off to do some work, I so he was looking serious when I asked about his day. It looks like I need to increase the number of times I do this, until my conversations are more mindful. The comment after the conversation was that it was great to be listened to so fully.

Which of the techniques will you use to sustain your practice?
Appling all my attention to what the other person says, and bing quiet, not judging or bringing in emotions. Also removing all the thoughts I have and stop the 'self-talk' when you start thinking about what you are going to say or do next. Repeating back what you heard in a sentence which adapts what you understood, is a good way to evaluate that the essence of the concepts has been communicated. This is a good learning tool, and helps make things clear.

Other musings?
To keep remoinding myself to find the some calm time and to use these skills, to set the mind on the daily path as it often doesn't take much for thte mind to wander.
